## 1. Pam Harris Numeracy & Number Sense Development

### Official Framework Overview

Pam Harris is a mathematics consultant, former high school teacher, and instructor at Texas State University who has spent over ten years researching how to develop mathematical reasoning and number sense in K-12 students. Her framework is rooted in the research-based principle that when students construct numerical relationships, they begin to believe mathematics is understandable—not a collection of abstract rules to memorize, but an arena in which they can reason using intuitive sense.

**Core Philosophy:** *Mathematics is about reasoning, not just getting answers. It is about developing mathematical reasoning and positioning all students as mathematical sense makers.*

### Key Pedagogical Principles

**1. Building on Student Thinking**
- Teachers listen closely to understand how students are thinking about numbers
- Instruction builds from what students already know and sparks for them
- The emphasis is on mathematical reasoning over answer-getting
- Students solve problems using what naturally emerges from their thinking, not rote procedures

**2. Problem Strings**
A problem string is a "purposefully designed sequence of related problems that helps students mentally construct numerical relationships and nudges them toward a major, efficient strategy." Problem strings allow students to:
- See relationships between problems
- Develop flexible thinking
- Construct increasingly efficient strategies naturally
- Apply mathematical reasoning to connected problem sets

**3. Core Numeracy Components**

**Subitizing:** The ability to instantly recognize the number of objects in a small set without counting. Subitizing is foundational because when it is strong, students move fluidly into:
- Composing numbers
- Understanding part-part-whole relationships
- Making sense of addition and subtraction

**Decomposition:** Breaking numbers apart in multiple ways using concrete objects, drawings, and eventually equations. Students should:
- Decompose numbers ≤10 in multiple ways
- Record decompositions using drawings and equations
- Understand relationships between decompositions (e.g., 5 = 2+3 = 4+1)

**Flexible Thinking:** Students develop flexible strategies rather than locked-in procedures, allowing them to solve increasingly complex problems with understanding.

### Unit/Lesson Structure

Harris has published several series of resources with grade-band specific structures:

**Developing Mathematical Reasoning Series (DMR)**
- K-2 volume: Foundational numeracy building
- 3-5 volume: Multi-digit operations and strategic reasoning
- Grades 6-8 volume: Numeracy expansion and algebraic thinking

Each grade-level book includes:
- Teaching strategies for foundational math concepts
- Mathematical models and representations
- Grade-specific lessons (with separate print and ebook versions)
- Numeracy Problem Strings (companion resources)

Companion volumes released/coming:
- K-2 grade band companion book (Spring 2026)
- Grades 3-5 companion book (Fall 2026)

### SWBAT Examples for K-5 Grade Levels

**Kindergarten:**
- SWBAT subitize sets of 1-4 objects without counting
- SWBAT recognize that numbers can be made by putting together smaller numbers (composition)
- SWBAT decompose small numbers (≤5) in multiple ways using objects and drawings
- SWBAT count with one-to-one correspondence for sets to 20

**Grade 1:**
- SWBAT quickly recognize (subitize) sets of 1-10 objects
- SWBAT decompose numbers to 10 in two or more ways and record with drawings/equations
- SWBAT make tens and use tens to solve addition/subtraction problems
- SWBAT represent and solve word problems using objects, drawings, and equations

**Grade 2:**
- SWBAT fluently add and subtract within 20 using mental strategies based on number relationships
- SWBAT understand place value for 2-digit numbers (tens and ones)
- SWBAT decompose numbers in multiple flexible ways to solve problems
- SWBAT solve word problems involving addition and subtraction using part-part-whole thinking

**Grade 3:**
- SWBAT build relationships between addition and multiplication (equal groups)
- SWBAT use number sense and relationships to solve multi-step word problems
- SWBAT develop and apply flexible strategies for multiplication and division
- SWBAT explain their reasoning using mathematical models

**Grade 4-5:**
- SWBAT multiply and divide fluently using flexible strategies and number relationships
- SWBAT solve word problems using multiple strategies and justify reasoning
- SWBAT build understanding of decimals and fractions through part-whole relationships
- SWBAT connect concrete models to abstract thinking and symbolic notation

### Assessment Approaches

Harris's assessment framework emphasizes:
- **Formative observation** of student thinking and reasoning during problem-solving
- **Problem strings** as both teaching and assessment tools, revealing how students are thinking
- **"Spark" identification:** Understanding what naturally draws each student toward efficient strategies
- **Growth over time:** Tracking development of flexible strategies and reasoning
- **Student-centered feedback:** Comments based on the mathematical reasoning observed, not just correctness

### Grade Band Focus Areas

**K-2 (Foundation):** Number sense, subitizing, decomposition, part-whole relationships, basic addition/subtraction reasoning

**3-5 (Efficiency):** Building flexibility with operations, place value understanding, beginning multiplication/division concepts, multi-digit operations

**6+ (Extension):** Rational number concepts, algebraic thinking, mathematical reasoning across domains

## 2. Jo Boaler Mathematical Mindset

### Official Framework Overview

Jo Boaler is the Nomellini-Olivier Professor of Education in Mathematics at Stanford University. The Mathematical Mindset Theory addresses the critical need to infuse mindset ideas into mathematics teaching, learning, and assessment. Boaler co-founded YouCubed, a Stanford research center funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, which provides free mindset math lessons used in over 100,000 schools worldwide.

**Core Philosophy:** *For mindset ideas to take root, students need to see mathematics as a subject of growth, which comes when questions are more open, and assessment provides feedback on learning.*

### Key Principles

**1. Growth Mindset & Neuroscience Integration**
- Mathematics ability is not fixed but grows with effort and practice
- The brain makes new connections when struggling with hard problems
- Mistakes and struggles are valuable opportunities for brain growth
- Explicit growth mindset messaging is integrated throughout instruction

**2. Low Floor, High Ceiling Tasks**
Open-ended tasks that:
- Are accessible to all students (low floor)
- Extend to high levels of complexity (high ceiling)
- Can be approached in multiple ways
- Encourage varied solution strategies and representations

**3. Visual & Multidimensional Thinking**
Based on neuroscience research, the approach emphasizes:
- Visual representations of mathematical ideas
- Multiple solution methods (graphical, symbolic, concrete, numerical)
- Pattern recognition and relational thinking
- Dimensional thinking that engages multiple areas of the brain

**4. Conceptual Understanding Over Speed**
- Mathematical depth over quick answers
- Understanding relationships between concepts
- Flexible thinking rather than procedural fluency alone
- Reasoning justified by students' own explanations

**5. Open Questions & Mathematical Reasoning**
- Questions with multiple entry points
- Open-ended investigations rather than closed drill
- Student generation of strategies and solutions
- Justification of mathematical thinking

### Unit/Lesson Structure

**Mindset Mathematics Series** (for K-8):
Published lesson collections for grades 2-8 that organize mathematics around "big ideas" with:
- Classroom activities designed around low-floor/high-ceiling tasks
- Visual and hands-on investigations
- Discussion prompts for mathematical reasoning
- Multiple solution strategies and representations
- Assessment integrated throughout

**YouCubed Online Resources:**
- Free lesson banks organized by grade and topic
- Professional development videos and tools
- Research-based teaching strategies
- Implementation guides for classroom use

### SWBAT Examples for K-8 (Mindset + Conceptual Focus)

**Grade K-1:**
- SWBAT decompose numbers in multiple ways and explain their thinking visually
- SWBAT use multiple strategies to solve addition problems and discuss why different strategies work
- SWBAT recognize that different ways of showing a number are equivalent
- SWBAT explain their thinking about quantity, subitizing, and counting

**Grade 2:**
- SWBAT solve addition and subtraction problems in multiple ways and explain why different strategies work
- SWBAT visualize and represent numbers using arrays, groups, and other models
- SWBAT investigate patterns and make conjectures about number relationships
- SWBAT discuss and justify their mathematical reasoning in small groups

**Grade 3:**
- SWBAT develop and communicate multiple strategies for multiplication and division
- SWBAT investigate and explain patterns in multiplication arrays and area models
- SWBAT solve open-ended problems using multiple representations (drawings, equations, models)
- SWBAT explain why a strategy works and when it is most efficient

**Grade 4-5:**
- SWBAT solve complex multi-step problems in multiple ways and justify their reasoning
- SWBAT visualize fraction and decimal concepts using area models, number lines, and other representations
- SWBAT investigate and communicate patterns and relationships in mathematical thinking
- SWBAT analyze different solution methods and discuss mathematical efficiency and elegance

**Grade 6-8:**
- SWBAT solve real-world problems using algebraic and geometric reasoning with multiple solution paths
- SWBAT create visual representations and algebraic models to explore mathematical relationships
- SWBAT justify mathematical claims and communicate reasoning with mathematical language
- SWBAT investigate mathematical patterns and make/test conjectures about relationships

### Growth Mindset Assessment Approaches

**Assessment for Learning (Formative Assessment):**
- Focuses students on learning and understanding, not just grades
- Provides diagnostic feedback identifying strengths, challenges, and next steps
- Replaces frequent timed testing with meaningful feedback
- Encourages students to see feedback as information for growth

**Key Assessment Principles:**
- Open questions allow students to show understanding in multiple ways
- Mistakes are analyzed for learning insights, not penalized
- Feedback is descriptive and growth-oriented, not comparative
- Students track growth over time in understanding, not speed
- Self-assessment and reflection are built into instruction

**Evidence of Impact:**
An 18-lesson teaching intervention using this framework raised students' test scores by an average of 50%, demonstrating measurable achievement gains alongside mindset development.

### How This Differs from Traditional Procedural Approach

| Traditional Procedural | Boaler's Mindset |
|---|---|
| Speed and accuracy emphasized | Understanding and reasoning emphasized |
| Timed tests, high-stakes grades | Diagnostic feedback, formative assessment |
| One correct method taught | Multiple strategies developed |
| Mistakes are failures | Mistakes are learning opportunities |
| Closed questions (right/wrong) | Open questions (multiple valid approaches) |
| Ability is fixed | Ability grows with effort |
| Procedural fluency is the goal | Conceptual understanding is the goal |
| Abstract symbol manipulation | Visual and relational thinking |

## 3. Wilson Reading System (WRS) - Complete Unit Structure

### Official Overview

The Wilson Reading System is an Orton-Gillingham-based, structured literacy program that teaches the structure of the English language directly using an integrated and sequential system of 12 Steps. WRS is designed for students in grades 2-12 and adults with word-level deficits who have not made sufficient progress through regular instruction or require more intensive Structured Literacy instruction due to language-based learning disabilities such as dyslexia.

**Core Philosophy:** *Explicit, systematic, sequential instruction in the alphabetic principle, phoneme-grapheme correspondences, syllable types, morphology, and connected text reading and spelling.*

### Official 12-Step Unit Structure

#### Steps 1-6: Foundation & Introduction to Morphology

**Step 1: Closed Syllables (3 sounds)**
- Focus: CVC words with short vowels in single-syllable closed words
- Sub-steps:
  - 1.1: Initial consonants (f, l, m, n, r, s, d, g, p, t) + vowels a, i, o (blending)
  - 1.2: Additional consonants (b, sh, h, j, c, k, ck, v, w, x, y, z, ch, th, qu, wh) + vowels u, e
  - 1.3: Practice with words like wish, chop, wet
  - 1.4: Double consonants (ll, ss, ff) in words like bill, kiss, call
  - 1.5: Word families (am, an) with words like ham, fan
  - 1.6: Suffix -s addition with words like bugs, chills

**Step 2: Closed Syllables (4-6 sounds)**
- Focus: Phonetically regular blends and digraphs
- Sub-steps:
  - 2.1: Final blends and digraphs (ang, ing, ong, ung, ank, ink, onk, unk) with words like bang, pink
  - 2.2: Initial blends (consonant clusters) like bled, past, steps
  - 2.3: Closed syllable exceptions (ild, ind, old, ost, olt) with words like mold, host
  - 2.4: Five-sound words with suffix -s like blend, trumps
  - 2.5: Three-letter blends (scr-, spr-, str-) with six-sound words like sprint, scrap

**Step 3: Multisyllabic Closed Syllables**
- Focus: Two-syllable words with two closed syllables
- Words progress from simple (rabbit, napkin) to more complex (submit, contract)

**Step 4: Open Syllables**
- Focus: Syllables ending in a vowel with long vowel sound
- Patterns: -pam (preach-), -me (theme), -go (ego)

**Step 5: Final Silent E**
- Focus: VCe syllables (vowel-consonant-e producing long vowel)
- Patterns: CaCe (made), CiCe (fine), CoCe (hope), CuCe (cute)
- Includes words with suffixes (-ing, -ed, -er, -est) added to CaCe bases

**Step 6: Open Syllables with y**
- Focus: -y at the end of words where y makes the long i or long e sound
- Patterns: happy, baby, family (y as long e); shy, my, fly (y as long i)

#### Steps 7-12: Advanced Patterns, Spelling Options, and Morphology

**Step 7: Vowel Teams**
- Focus: Digraphs and vowel teams making single sounds
- Patterns: ai/ay, oi/oy, ea, ee, oa, oo (two sounds), ou, au, aw
- Introduction to spelling options for the same sound

**Step 8: Syllable Division Patterns**
- Focus: How to divide multisyllabic words for accurate reading
- Includes VC/CV division and V/CV division patterns
- Complex words with 3-4 syllables

**Step 9: Contractions & r-Controlled Vowels**
- Focus: Contractions (it's, don't, can't)
- r-controlled vowels: ar, or, er, ir, ur in single and multisyllabic words

**Step 10: Double Vowel "D" (Tricky Vowel Combinations)**
- Focus: Words with oo, oa, ou making unexpected sounds in specific contexts
- Complex vowel patterns that don't fit regular digraph rules

**Step 11: Suffix Instruction with Base Word Changes**
- Focus: Morphology with changing base words
- Suffixes: -ing, -ed, -er, -est, -ly, -ment, -less, -ful with rules for:
  - Doubling consonants (running from run)
  - Dropping silent e (making from make)
  - Changing y to i (happier from happy)

**Step 12: Prefixes & Advanced Morphology**
- Focus: Prefixes and Greek combining forms
- Prefixes: un-, re-, pre-, dis-, mis-, over-, under-, out-, sub-, super-
- Greek roots and combining forms (phon-, graph-, tele-, geo-)
- Complex word building with multiple morphemes

### Lesson Structure

WRS follows a 10-part integrated lesson plan at a quick pace with constant teacher-student interaction:

**Block 1: Word Study & Phonics (Parts 1-5)** - 30 minutes
- Part 1: Sound Card Drill (phoneme identification)
- Part 2: Sound Sequencing (words containing the focus sounds)
- Part 3: Word Building (constructing words with phoneme cards)
- Part 4: Blending (reading words left to right)
- Part 5: Segmenting (pulling apart sounds in words)

**Block 2: Spelling & Writing (Parts 6-8)** - 30 minutes
- Part 6: Spelling Dictation (teacher says sound, student writes letter)
- Part 7: Word Dictation (teacher says word, student writes)
- Part 8: Sentence Dictation (teacher says sentence, student writes)

**Block 3: Fluency & Comprehension (Parts 9-10)** - 30 minutes
- Part 9: Story Reading (decodable text containing focus skills)
- Part 10: Comprehension (questions about the story)

**Full lesson = 90 minutes (can be split across days)**

### SWBAT by Step

**Step 1:**
- SWBAT identify and produce phoneme sounds for initial consonants f, l, m, n, r, s, d, g, p, t
- SWBAT identify and produce short vowel sounds a, i, o
- SWBAT blend phonemes into 3-sound CVC words
- SWBAT segment 3-sound CVC words into individual phonemes
- SWBAT read and spell phonetically regular 3-sound CVC words

**Step 2:**
- SWBAT identify and produce phoneme sounds for consonant digraphs (sh, ch, th, wh) and additional consonants
- SWBAT blend and read 4-6 sound words with consonant clusters
- SWBAT segment and spell words with final digraphs and blends
- SWBAT read and spell words with -ang, -ing, -ong, -ung, -ank, -ink patterns

**Step 3:**
- SWBAT read two-syllable words with two closed syllables
- SWBAT identify syllable boundaries in two-syllable words
- SWBAT segment two-syllable words by syllable
- SWBAT spell two-syllable closed syllable words with accuracy

**Step 4:**
- SWBAT identify open syllables in spoken and written words
- SWBAT read and spell words with open syllables making long vowel sounds
- SWBAT blend open syllables in multisyllabic words

**Step 5:**
- SWBAT identify VCe syllables in written words
- SWBAT read and spell words with silent final e making long vowels
- SWBAT apply VCe pattern to read and spell words with suffixes (-ing, -ed)

**Step 6:**
- SWBAT identify y as a vowel at the end of words
- SWBAT produce long i and long e sounds for y
- SWBAT read and spell words with y as a vowel

**Step 7:**
- SWBAT identify and read vowel digraph patterns (ai, ay, ea, ee, oa, oo, ou, au, aw)
- SWBAT spell words using vowel digraphs
- SWBAT recognize multiple spellings for the same sound (ai/ay, oi/oy)

**Step 8:**
- SWBAT divide multisyllabic words by syllable type
- SWBAT read 3-4 syllable words using syllable division patterns
- SWBAT spell multisyllabic words by syllable

**Step 9:**
- SWBAT read and spell contractions
- SWBAT identify and produce r-controlled vowel sounds (ar, or, er, ir, ur)
- SWBAT read and spell multisyllabic words with r-controlled vowels

**Step 10:**
- SWBAT read and spell words with "tricky" double vowel patterns
- SWBAT understand exceptions to digraph rules
- SWBAT distinguish between regular digraphs and exception patterns

**Step 11:**
- SWBAT apply suffixes to base words with rule changes (doubling, dropping e, changing y)
- SWBAT read and spell words with inflectional endings (-ing, -ed, -er, -est, -ly)
- SWBAT understand how spelling changes when adding suffixes

**Step 12:**
- SWBAT identify and read prefixes in words
- SWBAT identify Greek combining forms and roots
- SWBAT read and spell complex words with multiple morphemes
- SWBAT determine word meaning using prefix/root/suffix analysis

### Assessment Methods per Step

- **Phoneme Drill:** Immediate feedback on sound identification and production
- **Sound Sequencing Check:** Ability to identify sounds in words
- **Word Building/Reading:** Accuracy and automaticity with step-specific words
- **Dictation Assessment:** Spelling accuracy reflecting mastery of step patterns
- **Transfer to Connected Text:** Application to decodable passages
- **Fluency Checks:** Reading rate and accuracy on grade-level decodable passages

### Transfer/Application Expectations

- **To Fluency:** Reading with increasing accuracy and automaticity
- **To Spelling:** Applying phonics knowledge to spelling unfamiliar words
- **To Comprehension:** Understanding increasingly complex decodable text
- **To Connected Reading:** Application to literature, science, social studies texts at appropriate level
- **Cross-Curricular:** Using decoding skills to access content in all subjects

## 4. Just Words - Unit Breakdown

### Official Overview

Just Words is a word-level intervention program published by Wilson Language Training for students in grades 3-12+ who need explicit, systematic instruction in reading and spelling multisyllabic words. The program combines synthetic phonics with explicit instruction in total word structure, teaching all English language letter-sound correspondences, syllable patterns (single and multisyllabic), and common prefixes, roots, and suffixes.

**Core Philosophy:** *Accelerated study of word structure through systematic instruction in the six syllable types, common Latin roots, and morphology, using kinesthetic-tactile memory techniques for automatic reading and spelling.*

### Official 14-Unit Structure + 2 Bonus Units

Each unit spans approximately 2 weeks, with 14 core units plus 2 bonus units totaling approximately 30 weeks of instruction.

**Unit 1: Closed Syllables (Review/Reinforcement)**
- Focus: Single closed syllables as foundation for multisyllabic work
- Patterns: CVC, CVC with final digraphs, CVC with initial blends
- Demonstration Words: cat, bat, stop, bless, shelf
- Students learn kinesthetic-tactile marking system for syllables

**Unit 2: Closed Syllables + Open Syllables**
- Focus: Introduction to two-syllable words with closed + open pattern
- Patterns: VC|CV and V|CV division (e.g., diner, camel, tulip)
- Demonstration Words: napkin, robot, cabin, sofa
- Students identify syllable types and mark accordingly

**Unit 3: Closed Syllables + VCe**
- Focus: Two-syllable words combining closed and VCe syllables
- Patterns: CVC|VCe (e.g., sunshine, inside, refuse)
- Demonstration Words: make + ing = making, home + work = homework
- Introduction to base words and compounds

**Unit 4: Double Vowel "A" & "E" & Open Syllables**
- Focus: Vowel digraphs in first syllable + open second syllable
- Patterns: ea/ee + open (e.g., beaten, evening, reason)
- Demonstration Words: season, teacher, eagle, weaken
- Introduction to vowel team patterns

**Unit 5: Open Syllables + Closed Syllables (Reversal)**
- Focus: Open syllable first, closed second
- Patterns: V|CVC (e.g., napkin, pencil, campus)
- Demonstration Words: paper, baby, tiger, pivot
- Reinforces syllable division concepts

**Unit 6: r-Controlled Vowels + Open Syllables**
- Focus: r-controlled patterns with open second syllable
- Patterns: ar/or/er/ir/ur + open (e.g., carpenter, forest, person)
- Demonstration Words: quarry, corner, harvest, perfect
- Multisyllabic r-controlled patterns

**Unit 7: Final Syllable -le**
- Focus: The special syllable pattern _le (usually preceded by consonant)
- Patterns: ble, dle, gle, kle, ple, tle, etc. (e.g., table, little, simple)
- Demonstration Words: apple, bottle, castle, marble, puzzle
- Understanding that -le is a syllable type, not just a suffix

**Unit 8: Unchanging Base Word + Common Suffixes**
- Focus: Morphology - adding suffixes without changing base words
- Patterns: base + -ing, -ed, -er, -est, -ly, -ful, -less, -ness
- Demonstration Words: help + ing = helping; build + er = builder
- Students understand morphemes and meaning change with suffixes

**Unit 9: More Suffixes + Introduction to Prefixes**
- Focus: Expanded suffix study and beginning prefix patterns
- Patterns: un-, re-, pre-, dis-, mis- + root words
- Demonstration Words: redo, unfold, preview, disconnect, misspell
- Understanding how prefixes change meaning

**Unit 10: Latin Roots (Set 1)**
- Focus: Common Latin roots and their meanings
- Roots: aqua (water), audi (hear), bio (life), cred (believe), duc (lead), fact (make), form (shape), fract/frag (break)
- Demonstration Words: aquatic, audition, biology, credit, conduct, factory, reform, fraction
- Recognizing roots in complex words

**Unit 11: Latin Roots (Set 2) + Advanced Prefixes**
- Focus: Additional Latin roots and compound prefix use
- Roots: ject (throw), luc (light), manu (hand), path (feeling), port (carry), scrib/script (write), spec (look), struct (build)
- Prefixes: over-, under-, out-, sub-, super-, inter-, pre-
- Demonstration Words: inject, eject, lucid, manuscript, sympathy, export, describe, inspect, construct, submarine

**Unit 12: Syllable Type Review + Greek Combining Forms**
- Focus: Consolidation of syllable types + introduction to Greek forms
- Patterns: phon (sound), graph (write), tele (far), geo (earth), meter (measure), photo (light)
- Demonstration Words: telephone, geography, thermometer, photocopy, geophone
- Understanding how Greek forms combine to create meaning

**Unit 13: Three-Syllable & Four-Syllable Words**
- Focus: Longer words applying all previous concepts
- Patterns: Various syllable combinations, morpheme analysis
- Demonstration Words: celebration, photography, combination, multiplication, incredible
- Applying syllable division, morphology, and roots to longer words

**Unit 14: Review & Application (Units 1-13)**
- Focus: Consolidation and mastery of all taught concepts
- Assessment: Student performance across all syllable types, morphemes, and root words
- Demonstration Words: Cumulative selection from all previous units
- Prepared for Midterm Review (covers Units 1-7) and Final Exam (covers Units 1-14)

**Bonus Unit 1: Additional Multisyllabic Patterns**
- Focus: Less common but important patterns and extensions
- Application: Extended practice and enrichment

**Bonus Unit 2: Spelling Variations & Options**
- Focus: Multiple spellings for similar sounds and patterns
- Application: Preparing for unrestricted English spelling in diverse vocabulary

### SWBAT per Unit

**Unit 1:**
- SWBAT identify and mark closed syllables in single and multisyllabic words
- SWBAT read and spell single closed syllable words
- SWBAT apply kinesthetic marking system to indicate syllable types

**Unit 2:**
- SWBAT identify syllable division between closed and open syllables
- SWBAT read two-syllable words with CVC|CV and VC|CV patterns
- SWBAT spell two-syllable words using syllable understanding

**Unit 3:**
- SWBAT identify VCe syllables in multisyllabic words
- SWBAT read words combining closed and VCe syllables
- SWBAT spell words and apply inflectional endings (-ing) correctly

**Unit 4:**
- SWBAT identify and read vowel digraph patterns (ea, ee) in multisyllabic words
- SWBAT understand that vowel digraphs make single sounds
- SWBAT spell words with vowel digraphs and open second syllables

**Unit 5:**
- SWBAT identify and read multisyllabic words with open-closed syllable patterns
- SWBAT use syllable division to decode unfamiliar words
- SWBAT spell multisyllabic words using open-closed patterns

**Unit 6:**
- SWBAT identify r-controlled vowel patterns in multisyllabic words
- SWBAT read and spell words with r-controlled vowels
- SWBAT apply r-controlled understanding to longer, complex words

**Unit 7:**
- SWBAT identify -le as a syllable type (not just a suffix)
- SWBAT correctly divide words ending in -le
- SWBAT read and spell words with final -le syllables

**Unit 8:**
- SWBAT add common suffixes to base words without changing the base
- SWBAT understand how suffixes change word meaning and function
- SWBAT read and spell multisyllabic words with unchanged base + suffix

**Unit 9:**
- SWBAT add a variety of suffixes to base words
- SWBAT identify and read words with prefixes (un-, re-, pre-, dis-, mis-)
- SWBAT understand how prefixes change word meaning

**Unit 10:**
- SWBAT identify Latin roots within words
- SWBAT use Latin roots to determine word meaning
- SWBAT read and spell words containing Latin roots (aqua, audi, bio, cred, duc, fact, form, fract)

**Unit 11:**
- SWBAT identify and use additional Latin roots (ject, luc, manu, path, port, scrib, spec, struct)
- SWBAT recognize and apply advanced prefixes (over-, under-, out-, sub-, super-, inter-)
- SWBAT analyze multisyllabic words using combined morpheme knowledge

**Unit 12:**
- SWBAT identify Greek combining forms within words
- SWBAT use Greek forms to understand word meaning
- SWBAT read and spell words with Greek combining forms (phon, graph, tele, geo, meter, photo)

**Unit 13:**
- SWBAT read 3-4 syllable words using all syllable type and morpheme knowledge
- SWBAT apply syllable division, root analysis, and morpheme understanding to complex words
- SWBAT spell longer, complex words using structural understanding

**Unit 14:**
- SWBAT demonstrate mastery of all Units 1-13 concepts
- SWBAT apply syllable type knowledge, morpheme analysis, and root understanding to unfamiliar words
- SWBAT read and spell grade-level appropriate multisyllabic words with automaticity

### Grade Band Recommendations

| Grade Band | Entry Point | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Grades 3-4 | Unit 1 (or after assessment) | Foundation-building; moving through Units 1-6 |
| Grades 5-6 | Unit 4-5 (after assessment) | Morphology and basic Latin roots |
| Grades 7-8 | Unit 7-8 (after assessment) | Advanced morphology, prefix/suffix combinations |
| Grades 9-12 | Unit 10+ (after assessment) | Complex roots, combining forms, advanced applications |

Students progress through units based on mastery, not grade level. Typical progression is 2 weeks per unit, but can vary by student needs.

### Assessment Approaches

**Progress Checks:** Administered at beginning of each unit (starting Unit 2)
- Quick assessment of prerequisite skills
- Determines readiness for new unit concepts
- Informs grouping and pacing decisions

**Unit Tests:** Administered at end of each unit
- **Dictation Component:** Teacher dictates sound words, phrases, and sentences; students write independently
  - Evaluates spelling mastery of unit concepts
  - Students practice through Student Challenge Book dictation pages
- **Decoding Component:** Students read demonstration words and phrases from the unit
  - Evaluates automaticity of reading
  - Student Challenge Phrase Kit used for progress charting
  - Teacher records miscues on laminated tally sheets

**Midterm Review:** Covers Units 1-7
- Comprehensive assessment of foundational concepts
- Includes: nonsense word decoding, multisyllabic word marking, spelling, suffix addition, prefix/root matching
- Diagnostic of progress through first half of curriculum

**Final Exam:** Covers all Units 1-14
- Comprehensive assessment of entire curriculum
- Determines overall mastery of Just Words program
- Guides next-step instruction or program transition

**Progress Tracking:**
- Wilson Blue and Red Pens provided for students to graph their own progress
- Visual tracking supports student motivation and growth mindset
- Teachers monitor progress using classroom checklists

| 4-6 | Vowel teams, r-controlled | 2 syllables, pattern variety | reason, corner, teacher |
| 7-8 | -le syllables, simple morphology | 2-3 syllables, base + suffix | table, helping, helpful |
| 9-11 | Prefixes, Latin roots | 3-4 syllables, morpheme analysis | redo, aquatic, manuscript |
| 12-14 | Greek forms, complex words | 4+ syllables, multiple morphemes | telephone, celebration |

## 5. UFLI Foundations - Lesson/Unit Structure

### Official Overview

UFLI Foundations is a comprehensive phonologically informed literacy curriculum developed by the University of Florida Literacy Institute. It provides explicit, systematic instruction in phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension for kindergarten through grade 5. The program is based on structured literacy and science of reading principles, with careful scaffolding across grades to ensure deep conceptual understanding and automaticity.

**Core Philosophy:** *Systematic, explicit instruction in sound-symbol relationships that builds from foundational phonemic awareness through complex word structures, with built-in concept checks for progress monitoring and careful grade-level scaffolding.*

### Daily Lesson Structure (Eight-Step Routine)

Each UFLI Foundations lesson follows a consistent eight-step structure that moves from review/warm-up through application:

**Steps 1-4: Warm-Up & Review (15-20 minutes)**
These steps activate prior knowledge and reinforce previously taught concepts:

1. **Phonemic Awareness Drill**
   - Activities focused on sound manipulation without letters
   - Examples: Sound blending, isolation, deletion, substitution, segmentation
   - Prepares children to link sounds to letters

2. **Visual Drill**
   - Presentation of letter cards showing sound-letter correspondences
   - Teacher points to letters; students say the sounds
   - Rapid drill format for automaticity
   - Can include blending sequences

3. **Auditory Drill**
   - Teacher says sounds; students identify and write corresponding letters
   - Develops automaticity of letter-sound associations
   - Can include blending of sounds into syllables

4. **Blending Drill**
   - Connecting separated sounds into words
   - Scaffolding: Sound sequences → syllables → words
   - Building automaticity with blending

**Step 5: New Concept Introduction (10-15 minutes)**
This step introduces a new sound-letter correspondence, syllable pattern, or reading skill:

5. **New Concept**
   - Explicit instruction of new phoneme-grapheme correspondence OR new syllable pattern/concept
   - Includes sound production, letter identification, blending practice
   - Guided practice reading and spelling simple words with the new concept
   - Uses multi-sensory techniques (say, write, trace)

**Steps 6-8: Application & Transfer (15-20 minutes)**
These steps provide varied practice and text-level application:

6. **Word Work**
   - Reading and spelling words containing the new concept
   - Activities may include: word building, word cards, dictation, manipulatives
   - Extends new learning to more complex examples
   - Develops automaticity

7. **Irregular Words**
   - Explicit instruction in high-frequency irregular words (sight words)
   - Words that don't follow phonetic patterns (the, of, because, said)
   - Memory strategies for non-decodable words
   - Practiced in isolation and in text

8. **Connected Text**
   - Reading decodable passages and stories
   - Text features phonetically regular words (with new concept) and irregular words
   - Comprehension questions to monitor understanding
   - Fluency practice

### Concept Check Progression K-5

UFLI Foundations uses concept-based assessments called "Concept Checks" to monitor student understanding as they progress through lessons.

**What are Concept Checks?**
- Brief, embedded assessments within lessons
- Monitor understanding of specific phoneme-grapheme correspondences and syllable patterns
- Spelled-out versions of words students should be able to decode/encode
- Occur throughout lessons to gauge readiness to move forward

**Concept Check Progression by Grade:**

**Kindergarten Concept Checks:**
- Initial sounds (first letter-sound correspondences)
- Sound sequences and simple blending
- High-frequency irregular words
- Readiness indicators for letter-sound automaticity

**1st Grade Concept Checks:**
- Complete consonant and vowel sound correspondences
- CVC word reading and spelling
- Consonant digraphs and blends
- High-frequency sight words
- Simple decodable text

**2nd Grade Concept Checks:**
- CVCe pattern and vowel digraphs
- Consonant blends with digraphs
- Syllable division patterns
- Multisyllabic CVC|CVC words
- More complex decodable text

**3rd Grade & Beyond Concept Checks:**
- Complex syllable types (r-controlled, -tion, -le)
- Morpheme analysis (prefixes, suffixes, roots)
- Multisyllabic word decoding (3-4+ syllables)
- Grade-level complex text

### Lesson & Unit Organization K-5

**Overall Structure:**
UFLI Foundations lessons are numbered sequentially (Lessons 1-80 per the sequence), organized into "blocks" by concept:

**Kindergarten Block Organization:**
- **Lessons 1-34:** Alphabet Unit (individual letter-sound correspondences)
- **Lessons 35-53:** Digraphs & Blends Unit
- **Lessons 54-62:** VCe Unit
- Lessons organized for full-year kindergarten scope and sequence

**1st Grade Block Organization:**
- **Lessons 1-34:** Alphabet Unit (review + automaticity)
- **Lessons 35-53:** Digraphs & Blends Unit
- **Lessons 54-62:** VCe Unit
- **Lessons 63-75:** Advanced concepts
- Repeats key lessons from kindergarten with increased complexity and automaticity expectations

**2nd Grade Block Organization:**
- **Lessons 1-15:** Alphabet Unit (quick review for automaticity)
- **Lessons 16-45:** Vowel Teams & Digraphs Unit
- **Lessons 46-70:** Syllable Division & Morphology Unit
- **Lessons 71-80:** Complex patterns and advanced concepts

**3rd Grade & Beyond:**
- Building on foundational concepts
- Introduction of morphological elements (prefixes, suffixes, roots)
- Word part analysis for meaning
- Transfer to academic reading

### SWBAT Across K-5 Grade Bands

**Kindergarten:**
- SWBAT identify individual phoneme sounds and corresponding letters (a-z)
- SWBAT blend individual sounds into CVC words
- SWBAT segment CVC words into sounds
- SWBAT read and spell simple CVC words
- SWBAT recognize and say high-frequency irregular words (the, and, a, etc.)
- SWBAT read simple decodable text with CVC words and sight words
- SWBAT show understanding of the sound system and how letters represent sounds

**Grade 1:**
- SWBAT automatically produce phoneme-grapheme correspondences (automaticity goal)
- SWBAT read and spell consonant digraphs (ch, sh, th, wh) and initial blends (bl, st, etc.)
- SWBAT read and spell final blends and digraphs
- SWBAT read and spell CVC words with accuracy and increasing automaticity
- SWBAT read grade-level decodable text with accuracy and comprehension
- SWBAT spell consonant-vowel-consonant words correctly
- SWBAT recognize irregular words automatically in text
- SWBAT demonstrate fluent reading of decodable passages

**Grade 2:**
- SWBAT read words with vowel digraphs (ea, ee, ai, oa, etc.) with automaticity
- SWBAT read and spell VCe (silent e) words
- SWBAT identify syllable boundaries in two-syllable words
- SWBAT read two-syllable words using VC|CV and V|CV patterns
- SWBAT read and comprehend grade-level decodable text with fluency
- SWBAT spell multisyllabic CVC|CVC words correctly
- SWBAT recognize irregular words automatically
- SWBAT demonstrate oral reading fluency with 80+ WPM

**Grade 3:**
- SWBAT read and spell words with r-controlled vowels (ar, or, er, ir, ur)
- SWBAT read 2-3 syllable words with varied syllable types (closed, open, VCe, digraphs)
- SWBAT identify syllable type within multisyllabic words
- SWBAT use syllable division to decode unfamiliar multisyllabic words
- SWBAT identify and use prefixes and suffixes to understand word meaning
- SWBAT read grade-level text with fluency (100+ WPM) and comprehension
- SWBAT spell grade-level words correctly
- SWBAT recognize how word parts change word meaning

**Grade 4-5:**
- SWBAT analyze multisyllabic words using syllable types and morphemes
- SWBAT identify word roots and affixes and use them for word meaning
- SWBAT read grade-level complex text with fluency and comprehension
- SWBAT spell grade-level words using morpheme understanding
- SWBAT demonstrate automaticity with all previous concepts
- SWBAT apply decoding strategies to subject-specific text (science, social studies, math)
- SWBAT read at appropriate grade-level fluency (115+ WPM) with expression

### How Concepts Build from K Through 5

**Kindergarten Foundation:**
Sound-letter relationships introduced one at a time; focus on understanding the alphabetic principle

↓

**Grade 1 - Automaticity & Combination:**
Sound-letter automaticity developed; consonant digraphs and blends introduced; CVC reading/spelling mastery

↓

**Grade 2 - Expansion & Multisyllabic:**
Vowel teams, VCe, and syllable types introduced; two-syllable word reading begins; fluency develops

↓

**Grade 3 - Application & Morphology:**
Complex syllable types, r-controlled vowels; morpheme awareness begins; transition to grade-level text

↓

**Grade 4-5 - Mastery & Transfer:**
All syllable types automatic; morphological analysis for word meaning; fluent reading of complex grade-level text across domains

**Key Principle:** UFLI Foundations *repeats key lessons across more than one grade level* (e.g., Lesson 44 in K and 1st grade) to support long-term retention and move from explicit instruction to automatic application.

### Assessment System

**Fluency Checks:**
- Unit assessments using decodable passages
- Measure oral reading fluency (ORF)
- Include words/patterns taught in the unit
- Provide overall picture of reading development

**Progress Monitoring Spelling Assessments:**
- Evaluate sound segmentation ability
- Assess spelling accuracy of words containing taught concepts
- Track growth in phoneme-grapheme knowledge

**Intervention Placement Test:**
- Validated placement tool for determining starting lesson
- Used when implementing UFLI Foundations as intervention
- Prevents starting too high (frustration) or too low (boredom)

**Concept Checks (Built-in):**
- Teacher observation during lessons
- Informal assessment of concept understanding
- Guides pacing decisions (when to move forward vs. review)

## 6. Bridges Math Intervention - Cycle Maps & Structure

### Official Overview

Bridges Intervention is a K-5 mathematics intervention program published by The Math Learning Center. It provides small-group, targeted instruction and ongoing progress monitoring for essential K-5 mathematics skills and concepts. The program is designed to complement regular math instruction and align with Response to Intervention (RTI) and Multi-Tiered System of Support (MTSS) frameworks.

**Core Philosophy:** *Strengths-based instruction starting with concrete manipulatives, moving to representational models and mental strategies, with continuous progress monitoring and assessment for learning.*

### Official Program Structure

Bridges Intervention is organized into **9 volumes** containing 8-13 modules each, for a total of 40-65 sessions per volume.

**Overall Organization:**
- **Volumes 1-3:** K and Grade 1 foundations
- **Volumes 4-7:** Grades 2-4 core operations
- **Volumes 8-9:** Grades 3-5 fractions and advanced concepts

**Module Structure:**
Each module contains **5 sessions** (approximately 30 minutes each):
- Sessions 1-4: Teaching activities, games, guided practice
- Session 5: Quick assessment (progress monitoring)

**Session Components:**
- Warm-up activity (5-7 minutes)
- Game or core activity (12-15 minutes)
- Guided practice page (5-8 minutes)

### Instructional Approach: Concrete → Representational → Abstract (CRA)

Bridges Intervention uses a **strengths-based approach** that:
1. Begins with **manipulatives** (concrete objects, blocks, counters)
2. Progresses to **2-D representations** (drawings, diagrams, number lines)
3. Transitions to **mental images and symbolic notation** (numbers and operations)

This progression honors students' thinking and builds conceptual understanding before procedural fluency.

### Grade Band Specific Volumes & Domains

**Kindergarten Focus - Volume 1 & 2**
- **Domain:** Counting & Cardinality (K)
- **Concepts:** Counting sequences, cardinality (one-to-one correspondence), comparing quantities
- **Modules:** Activities with number recognition, subitizing, counting practices, comparing sets
- **Sessions:** 8-13 modules per volume

**Grade 1 Focus - Volumes 2 & 3**
- **Domain:** Operations & Algebraic Thinking (K-4), Number & Operations in Base Ten (K-5)
- **Concepts:** Addition and subtraction within 10, then within 20, part-whole relationships
- **Modules:** Developing meaning for addition/subtraction, building fluency strategies
- **Sessions:** 40-65 sessions total across volumes

**Grade 2 Focus - Volumes 3 & 4**
- **Domain:** Operations & Algebraic Thinking (K-4), Number & Operations in Base Ten (K-5)
- **Concepts:** Addition and subtraction within 20 and 100, place value understanding
- **Modules:** Strategies for addition/subtraction, beginning two-digit computation
- **Sessions:** Targeted to major clusters

**Grade 3 Focus - Volumes 4 & 5**
- **Domain:** Operations & Algebraic Thinking (K-4), Number & Operations in Base Ten (K-5)
- **Concepts:** Multiplication and division meaning, multidigit arithmetic
- **Modules:** Developing multiplication/division, two-digit and three-digit computation
- **Sessions:** Targeted to major clusters

**Grade 4-5 Focus - Volumes 6 & 7**
- **Domain:** Operations & Algebraic Thinking (K-4), Number & Operations in Base Ten (K-5), Number & Operations: Fractions (3-5)
- **Concepts:** Multiplication/division fluency, multidigit fluency, fraction concepts
- **Modules:** Fluency with multiplication/division (within 100), multidigit fluency, fractions
- **Sessions:** Targeted to major clusters

**Grade 3-5 Advanced - Volumes 8 & 9**
- **Domain:** Number & Operations: Fractions (3-5)
- **Concepts:** Fraction understanding, fraction operations
- **Modules:** Fraction concepts (equal parts, comparing, ordering), fraction operations
- **Sessions:** 40-65 sessions addressing major clusters

### SWBAT Examples per Volume/Cycle

**Volume 1 (Kindergarten):**
- SWBAT count forward from a given number within the range 1-20
- SWBAT count objects and tell "how many" (cardinality)
- SWBAT compare two numbers and identify which is greater/less
- SWBAT recognize sets of 1-5 objects without counting (subitizing)
- SWBAT demonstrate one-to-one correspondence when counting objects

**Volume 2 (Kindergarten-Grade 1):**
- SWBAT solve addition problems within 5-10 using objects or drawings
- SWBAT understand that addition involves putting two groups together
- SWBAT represent addition using objects, pictures, and equations
- SWBAT find number bonds within 10 (two numbers that make a 10)
- SWBAT show fluency with addition combinations within 5, progressing to 10

**Volume 3 (Grade 1-2):**
- SWBAT solve addition and subtraction problems within 20 using strategies (counting on, making 10)
- SWBAT apply strategies for addition/subtraction to problems within 100
- SWBAT model addition and subtraction with manipulatives, drawings, and equations
- SWBAT build automaticity with addition/subtraction facts to 20
- SWBAT understand the inverse relationship between addition and subtraction

**Volume 4 (Grade 2-3):**
- SWBAT add and subtract two-digit numbers using place value strategies
- SWBAT understand tens and ones place value
- SWBAT apply multiple strategies (regrouping, decomposition, number lines)
- SWBAT show flexibility in choosing efficient strategies
- SWBAT verify answers using different methods

**Volume 5 (Grade 3-4):**
- SWBAT represent multiplication as equal groups and/or arrays
- SWBAT develop meaning for multiplication and division
- SWBAT solve multiplication problems within 100 using strategies (arrays, area models, repeated addition)
- SWBAT demonstrate understanding of division as sharing or grouping
- SWBAT build fluency with multiplication/division facts

**Volume 6 (Grade 4-5):**
- SWBAT multiply two-digit and multi-digit numbers using place value strategies
- SWBAT divide multi-digit numbers by single-digit divisors
- SWBAT develop and apply fluency strategies for multiplication and division within 100
- SWBAT solve multi-step word problems involving multiplication and division
- SWBAT explain and justify strategies using mathematical models

**Volume 7 (Grade 4-5):**
- SWBAT fluently multiply and divide within 100
- SWBAT apply multiplication and division to real-world situations
- SWBAT develop algebraic thinking through multiplication/division patterns
- SWBAT analyze and compare different strategies for efficiency
- SWBAT explain mathematical thinking with accuracy and clarity

**Volume 8 (Grade 3-5):**
- SWBAT identify fractions as equal parts of a whole
- SWBAT read and write fractions (1/2, 1/3, 1/4, etc.)
- SWBAT model fractions using area models, number lines, and manipulatives
- SWBAT compare and order fractions with same/different denominators
- SWBAT demonstrate understanding that same-size fractional parts differ depending on whole size

**Volume 9 (Grade 4-5):**
- SWBAT add and subtract fractions with like denominators
- SWBAT solve word problems involving fractions
- SWBAT apply fraction understanding to measurement contexts
- SWBAT connect fraction operations to whole number operations
- SWBAT demonstrate understanding of mixed numbers and improper fractions

### Assessment Checkpoints & Progress Monitoring

**Session 5 Quick Assessments:**
Every 5th session includes a quick progress monitoring assessment:
- Brief (5-8 minutes), focused on current module concepts
- Provides immediate data to guide instruction
- Determines whether to move forward, revisit, or provide additional support
- Results inform grouping and next session planning

**Placement Assessments:**
- Determine student starting point in intervention program
- Prevent students from beginning too high (frustration) or too low (boredom)
- Guide initial grouping decisions

**Module/Cycle Assessments:**
- More comprehensive check at end of major instructional units
- Shows application and transfer of concepts taught
- Informs progression decisions (ready for next volume, needs review, needs different focus)

**Progress Monitoring Principles:**
- **Data-Driven Decisions:** Assessment results guide instructional grouping, pacing, and focus
- **Consistent Scoring:** Grade-level teams score assessments the same way for reliable data
- **Collaborative Planning:** Data reviewed with colleagues to develop targeted instruction plans
- **Activity Selection:** Specific activities or entire sessions may be repeated based on assessment data

### Progression from K-5

**K Focus:** Number awareness, cardinality, comparing, subitizing → Grade 1 Foundation

↓

**Grade 1 Focus:** Addition/subtraction meaning within 10-20 → Grade 2 Extension

↓

**Grade 2 Focus:** Addition/subtraction fluency within 100, place value understanding → Grade 3 Application

↓

**Grade 3 Focus:** Multiplication/division meaning, beginning multidigit strategies → Grade 4 Fluency

↓

**Grade 4-5 Focus:** Multiplication/division fluency, multidigit operations, fractions → Foundation for upper elementary and beyond

**Key Design:** Each volume addresses major clusters identified within national standards. Intervention is targeted to these high-leverage concepts so students develop deep understanding and fluency with concepts essential for future mathematics success.
